The Problem with Performance Goals in a Crisis

Performance goals sound like this:

"Deliver 10,000 rations by Friday."

In a stable environment, this works. The road is open. Fuel is available.

In a conflict zone, the road closes. The fuel disappears.

The team cannot meet the goal. They feel like failures. They lose confidence.

This is not a team problem. This is a goal problem.

The Solution: Learning Intentions First

A learning intention sounds like this:

"Learn how to deliver rations when the main road is closed."

Performance GoalLearning Intention
"Deliver 10,000 rations by Friday" "Learn how to deliver rations without a road"
"Fix the broken vehicle in 24 hours" "Learn how to borrow a vehicle from the community"
"Find a new interpreter by the end of the day" "Learn how to communicate without an interpreter"
💡 Note: Learning intentions do not mean giving up on performance goals. They are the "bridge" to reach them. In conflict zones, clinging to rigid goals in a changing environment leads to paralysis; focusing on learning gives you the alternative path to achieve those goals. You are not abandoning your goal, you are finding the best way to reach it.
💡 First, learn the terrain. Then, set the target.

📝 Practice: Convert to Learning Intentions

Instructions: For each scenario, write a learning intention. 0/4 completed

📍 Scenario 1 (Flooding, Lebanon)

A bridge collapsed. Your team cannot reach the distribution point.

Performance Goal: "Deliver food supplies by tomorrow."

📍 Scenario 2 (Internet shutdown, Syria)

All communication lines are down. You cannot contact HQ.

Performance Goal: "Get HQ approval by noon."

📍 Scenario 3 (Staff arrest, Kyiv)

Your interpreter was arrested. You cannot communicate with partners.

Performance Goal: "Find a new interpreter by the end of the day."

📍 Scenario 4 (Vehicle breakdown, DRC)

Your only vehicle broke down. Team is waiting to go to the field.

Performance Goal: "Fix the vehicle within 6 hours."

Suggested Learning Intentions:

  • Lebanon (Flooding): "Learn how to reach beneficiaries using alternative routes or local boats."
  • Syria (Internet shutdown): "Learn how to make autonomous decisions without HQ approval."
  • Kyiv (Staff arrest): "Learn how to communicate using gestures, drawings, or community volunteers."
  • DRC (Vehicle breakdown): "Learn who in the community has a vehicle we can borrow."
Key Takeaway
Performance goals measure success.
Learning intentions enable success.

In a crisis, start with learning. The performance will follow.
