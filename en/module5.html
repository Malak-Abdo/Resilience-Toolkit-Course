Why Habits, Not Motivation

In a conflict zone, motivation comes and goes.

One day you feel energetic. The next day, you feel frustrated, can't sleep, and receive bad news like team reductions.

Habits are different. They don't require motivation. They run automatically when triggered.

High-performing leaders don't rely on willpower alone. These leaders design habit loops for their most common crises.

The Habit Loop (Cue → Routine → Reward)

From the McKinsey methodology

ElementWhat it meansExample
Cue What starts the habit "The thermometer reaches 45°C at 9:00 AM"
Routine The automatic behavior "I ask: What critical tasks can be done before 11:00 AM?"
Reward What you feel "Control. My team is safe. Work is still moving."

Example: Completed Habit Loops

CrisisCueRoutineReward
Fuel shortage "The fuel gauge hits zero" "I ask: What works without fuel?" "Agency. We are not stuck."
Staff departure "A key staff member resigns" "I ask: Who can take part of this task?" "Curiosity. Not despair."
Extreme heat "The thermometer reaches 45°C at 9:00 AM" "I ask: What can we accomplish before 11:00 AM?" "Control. My team is safe."
